Pauli Paananen

Violinist, Freelancer, Teacher

Pauli Paananen is a Dutch-Finnish violinist based in Amsterdam

He was 5 years old when he started playing the violin as a pupil of his parents. At the age of 7 he became a student of Hans Scheepers in Amstelveen and now studies with Peter Brunt in his third year at the Conservatorium van Amsterdam. He has also received masterclasses from a.o. Anatoli Melnikov at the ‘Kangasniemen Musiikkiviikot’ summer course in Finland and several teachers at the Sibelius-Academy in Helsinki. Pauli has played chamber music with his family and friends since he was a child, and won a third prize at the Inter Provinciaal Muziek Concours together with the Helders Strijktrio in 2008. At the age of 14 he won a first prize at the Artiance Concours in Alkmaar, and was admitted to the Rabo Talentenlab talent program. Pauli has also played in several youth orchestras, among others in the Noord-Hollands Jeugdorkest, conducted by Bas Pollard, for six years, three of which as the concertmaster. He was concertmaster of the conscript band of the Finnish defense forces in 2021, and is a member of the Nationaal JeugdOrkest, where he has played as principal second violin. He is currently playing on a violin that was built for him by master luthier Annelies Steinhauer.
